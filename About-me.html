About me

I am Antonio Guillén Martínez, graduate of Computer Science and Mathematics at the University of Murcia.

From a young age, I discovered my passion for video game development. My first encounter was at the age of 10 when I realized that you could simulate collisions in PowerPoint by adding hyperlinks to geometric shapes. Using this tool, I developed small mini-games, marking the beginning of a hobby that I continue to pursue in my free time.

Today, I am proficient in several programming languages, including C, C++, Java, Python, and GDScript (Godot's programming language). I also have experience with game engines such as Godot and Unity. I consider myself a self-taught person, with a great curiosity and eagerness to continue learning and improving my skills.

Previous experience

Actualmente he tenido dos trabajos previos, siendo uno de ellos en una empresa de videojuegos:

CyberDataLab at the University of Murcia (CDL - UMU)  CyberDataLab is a research laboratory affiliated with the University of Murcia. My role in this lab involves modeling, generating, and simulating a social network, where users are simulated agents using large language models to study disinformation in this area.  

Microsoft | Activision Blizzard King internship                                         During my internship, I developed a system to detect problematic game levels by analyzing key performance metrics. This project involved close collaboration with level designers to identify areas for improvement and optimize the overall level design for a better player experience.
